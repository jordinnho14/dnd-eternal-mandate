## Quest Text
The local midwife and herbalist, Adabra Gwyn, lives by herself in a stone windmill on a hill a few miles North of Phandalin. She usually comes to Phandalin on Tuesday's and Thursday's to treat the sick and sell ointments and potions. She left on Thursday and hasn't been heard of since. Head out to Umbrage Hill and check on her. If possible, urge her to relocate to Phandalin permanently - I have a feeling we'll have need of her in the coming days.

#### On arrival
*"The stone windmill atop Umbrage Hill has seen better days, but right now, its heavy oak door is buckling under a relentless assault. A massive beast—part lion, part bat, with a terrifying, spiked scorpion tail—is frantically hurling itself against the wood. From the second-story window, a fiery dwarven woman is throwing sparking, illegal magical firecrackers at the beast, screaming at it to 'shove off!' The monster doesn't roar back; instead, in a raspy, booming voice, it speaks perfect Common: 'I am STARVING, witch! Open the door!'"*

- Gwyn knows that the mandate processing plants have flooded the hill forests (manticore hunting grounds) with toxic sludge, killing the deer and boar they would normally feed on.

### 2. The Threat (Level 3 Balanced)

This Manticore isn't evil; it's desperate. Overseer Vex’s toxic sludge ruined the local hunting grounds.
- **The Twist (The Mate):** The starving male at the door is a distraction. If the party attacks, his **Mate** is circling high in the rainy clouds, waiting to dive-bomb the party's backline.

### 3. The Player Approaches (How they handle it)

**Option A: Tactical Combat (Violence)**
- If the party attacks, the male takes to the sky, and the hidden female drops from the clouds onto the Wizard/Rogue.
- **Win Condition:** They are hungry, not suicidal. If either drops below 20 HP, they flee toward the mountains.

**Option B: The Diplomatic Bribe (Food)**
- The party can offer their rations or freshly hunted meat.
- **Mechanic:** DC 13 Persuasion or Animal Handling. If successful, it complains the meat is stringy, but takes it and flies off to find a new territory.

**Option C: The Mercenary Deal (Weaponizing the Beast)** Manticores are Lawful Evil—they are greedy and transactional. Smart players can strike a deal:
- **The "Enemy of My Enemy":** Tell it there is a fat, well-fed Mandate supply caravan two miles down the road. (DC 15 Persuasion/Deception).
- **The Guard Dog:** Tell it to nest in the ruined bell tower of Tresendar Manor (the players' future base) in exchange for eating any Mandate tax collectors that come knocking.
- **The Airstrike:** Promise it a steady supply of meat if it gives the players a way to call it (a whistle/bonfire) to aid them in one future boss fight.

### 4. The Apothecary & The Reward
Once the beast is dealt with, Adabra Gwynn unbars the door.
- **The Vibe:** She is a pragmatic, fugitive dwarf who refuses to stop brewing magic just because the Mandate said so. She thinks she is safer out here than in town.
- **The Pitch:** The players must convince her the town is dying without medicine. A **DC 12 Persuasion** check gets her to pack her bags.
- **The Loot:** She gives the party **two Potions of Healing** (worth 100gp).
- **The Town Upgrade:** When she returns to Phandalin, she claims the empty market cart in the town square. The players now have a permanent potion vendor, and the town feels safer!
