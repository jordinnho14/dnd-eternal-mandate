### OUTLINE
- Set the scene, eternal mandate, [[Lords Alliance]] gathering to attack, nip at the bud
- Start in tent outside [[Aegis Hold]], heavy rain, preparing for great assault
- Sub-lieutenant Thaddeus [[Pip]], takes a roll call, explains the plan (they need to take out a steam powered mortar on a ridge overlooking the castle, while the rest of the army makes its assault)
- Goes over plan, but says they have a few minutes to kill, asks about combat experience or why they joined up
- Assault on the mortar (each round explain what they see happening below)
- They succeed, but its a trap, Lords alliance all killed, characters knocked into the river below by the blast
- They awake, in an inn, [[Pip]] is there with them
- Intro to [[Toblen Stonehill]] (inkeep/mayor)
	- Update on the situation
	- Tells them about [[Phandalin]] and the surrounding area
	- Brings up the unfortunate reality of their medical bills, and suggests doing the quest board to get by


### Medical Bills
- They owe 5000 Gold pieces
- Itemised:
	- Rent & secrecy - 2k gp "Do you know how much I had to bribe the mandate tax collector so he wouldn't look in my cellar for twenty-four straight months?"
	- **Medical Care:** 2,500 gp. _"Sister Garaele doesn't work for free, and black-market healing potions cost 50 gold a pop! You drank three a week just to stop coughing up black smoke!"_
	- **The 'Pip' Tax:** 500 gp. _"And this idiot has been eating all me premium salted pork and breaking my barstools for two years while waiting for you to wake up! Never showed much interest in making any money or taking on any jobs, said he was too distressed while you lot were suffering down there"

### What happened in the 2 years
Toblen doesn't know everything that's happened, just from Phandalin's point of view
- **The early days**: *"We're quite out of the way here in Phandalin, a hokey little frontier town sprung up in the mountains yknow, other than you lot washing up out of the river, and the ruddy great green light show that one night, it was mostly just rumours coming out of the bigger cities - Neverwinter, Waterdeep, the like. But life kept on going around here, folk just earning a living. The old mayor, Harbin, he told us to ignore the rumours and just get on with things.*
- **Ripple effects**: "But soon, the letters from Neverwinter dried up - we used to get most of our supplies out of there, and suddenly there was nothing. No post, no pigeons, nothing. We sent a messenger boy by horse there to see if he could get an idea of what was going on, but he never came back... *He shudders*... my nephew that was, good lad, gods only know what's happened to him. We got a trickle of refugees from there, telling tales of undead husks, and clockwork monsters, but I don't know what to believe.
- **Recently**: "It was quite for a while really, times have been hard without the supplies from Neverwinter, but we've made do. That is 'til the mandate heard about the quartz mines. Some dwarven lads have been digging up quartz in the mountains somewhere, and word must have got round cause we've been seeing more and more of them mandate lads around, asking lots of questions, demanding taxes - not money mind you, odd things, copper, glass lenses and the like, and they'd keep asking if there were any Elves in town, luckily none are actually in Phandalin, but there are a lot of them in small settlements around the place. It's not like Neverwinter, here, it's big and empty and rural, tiny villages and settlements all through these hills, I can't imagine the mandate is going to have much luck taking over."
- **Himself as mayor**: "I didn't think too much of this Eternal Mandate lot at first, but when they came asking for taxes, our old mayor told em to sling their hook, and they gunned him down in the street, with some kind of exploding crossbow thing, never seen anything the like. Anyway since then, I've taken on the duty of acting mayor, "

### Introducing the quests board
- Toblen tells them about med bills, but agrees that they can help out around the town, as with Neverwinter being shut off, and the increasing presence of the Eternal Mandate, they could use some extra help - there's a jobs board just outside the inn at the town square, and he'll escort them out to take a look. 
- When they get out there, introduce [[The Iron Eclipse]]
	- *the daylight abruptly vanishes. A shadow, sharp and absolute, swallows the entire town of Phandalin.*
	- *The muddy water in the rain barrels begins to vibrate. A low, bone-rattling hum of massive turbines hits you right in the chest. The loose shingles on the tavern roof rattle and slide into the mud.*
	- You look up. Grinding through the storm clouds is a floating fortress—a jagged, city-block-sized slab of black iron and spinning brass gyros. It doesn't fly gracefully; it crushes the air beneath it, spewing thick, sulfurous smog from massive exhaust vents.
	- Suddenly, a deafening, metallic voice booms from the belly of the dreadnought. It echoes off the mountains with heavy, mechanical distortion:
	- *'CITIZENS OF THE SWORD COAST. REJOICE. YOU ARE NOW UNDER THE PROTECTION OF THE ETERNAL MANDATE. CEASE ALL WOODLAND ACTIVITIES AND REPORT TO THE PROCESSING TENTS. OBEY AND THERE WILL BE ORDER"
	- It flies past
	- They also meet [[Halia Thornton]], as the only person who didn't immediately flee inside.
	- _"Toblen finally let the basement rats out, huh? Heard you were part of the team who crapped the bed against these guys a couple of years ago. Pretty embarrassing. Better get used to that thing flying overhead, we're gonna be seeing a lot more of it"_ - she looks you up and down. "*If you'r ever interested in standing up to these metal goons, come see me at the old miner's exchange, I'll be out of town for a few days, important business, but if I make it back in one piece, maybe we can help each other* "
- **IMPORTANT - REMEMBER TO MAKE PIP STAY** Have Pip dramatically claim he needs to "stay behind to coordinate intelligence" or "guard the rear flank" (meaning he is just going to hide under a table and drink ale). The players will roll their eyes, laugh, and happily leave him behind.

#### If the group need a carrot or a stick to do the quests:
- **Plan A (The Carrot):** Toblen gives them the itemized bill. He's stressed, he's sweaty, but he appeals to their honor. _"I kept you alive for two years. I'm broke. Please, just do a couple of these bounties so I don't lose my tavern."_ * _If the players say:_ "Wow, that sucks. Yeah, we'll help you out." $\rightarrow$ Boom. You go straight to the Bounty Board and introduce the _Iron Eclipse_.
    
- **Plan B (The Stick):** * _If the players say:_ "Screw you, old man, we're Level 3 badasses. We're just going to walk out the front gate and leave." $\rightarrow$ That is when Toblen drops the hammer. _"Walk out the gate? In that Alliance armor? I've heard the mandate has set up checkpoints on the major roads and settlements. You'll need transit papers to get through, now I can help you out with that, I've got the connections, but you'll need to do something for me first.
