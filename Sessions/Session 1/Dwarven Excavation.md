## ⛏️ Quest 1: The Quartz Claim (Dwarven Excavation)

**Quest Text:** Dwarf prospectors have discovered a large quartz vein in the mountains east of Leilon, and southwest of here, at the end of a large canyon, and have begun excavating. The Eternal Mandate are searching for this mine for unknown reasons, but the dwarves must be warned. Take the warning to them, and return to Mayor Stonehill. Reward: 100gp

---

### **Zone 1: The Canyon Entrance (The Hostage Situation)**

As the party approaches the canyon, they hear the deafening hiss of steam and furious Dwarven cursing.

> **Read-Aloud / The Scene:** _"Peeking around the canyon wall, you see the Mandate working on a crude, smog-spewing steam-drill right into the rock face, which is rumbling kind of alarmingly. Off to the side, two Dwarf prospectors (Dazlyn & Norbus) are kneeling in the mud, hands bound with heavy iron cuffs. A Mandate [[Shock troopers | Shock-Trooper]] stands over them with a clipboard, while a ticking [[Clockwork Hounds|Clockwork Hound]] paces back and forth."_
> 
> **Dazlyn (Yelling):** _"Ye tin-can bastards! We filed our claim! This is our rock!"_ **Mandate Trooper (Deadpan):** _"Neverwinter jurisdiction is dissolved. All subterranean chronal assets belong to the Eternal Mandate. Cease vocalisation, or the hound will remove your vocal cords."_

- **The Drill Hazard:** The steam-drill is leaking. If a player shoots the pressure valve (AC 13), it explodes for **2d6 fire (steam) damage** in a 10-foot radius.
    
- **The Clue:** The Trooper's clipboard reads: _"Vein located. Chronal resonance is high. Send the Husks in to crack the geode."_
    

**2/3 shock troopers, 1 clockwork hound**

---

### **Zone 2: The Main Hall (The Strip Mine)**

*You step into the dark, echoing remains of an ancient Dwarven temple. The Mandate has completely defiled it, bolting heavy iron scaffolding right into the carved pillars. The walls glitter with veins of raw, uncut quartz. The only sound is the slow, rhythmic CLANG... CLANG... CLANG of heavy pickaxes striking stone. In the dim light, you see pale, rotting figures mindlessly swinging the tools. They are dead men, moving with unnatural, jerky stiffness, their glassy eyes fixed blankly on the rock.*

**The Dimensions & Lighting**

- **Size:** It is a massive, rectangular chamber, about 40 feet wide and 60 feet long.
    
- **Lighting:** The room is plunged in darkness, broken only by harsh, flickering green light coming from three alchemical lanterns the Mandate has hung on the walls.
    
- **The Ceiling:** 20 feet high, supported by four thick, intricately carved Dwarven stone pillars.
    

**The Terrain & Interactive Elements (What's in the room?)** The Mandate has turned this ancient, sacred temple into a brutal industrial strip mine.

- **The Scaffolding:** Heavy, rusted iron scaffolding has been bolted directly into the stone pillars. _Mechanic: A player can climb the scaffolding (no check required) to get a height advantage for ranged attacks, but if a pillar takes 10 or more damage from a spell or explosive, the scaffolding collapses, dropping anyone on it for 1d6 bludgeoning damage._
    
- **The Minecart:** In the center of the room sits a heavy iron minecart on a set of freshly laid, temporary tracks. It is half-full of glowing quartz rubble. _Mechanic: The tracks are on a slight downward slope toward the back of the room. A player can use an Action to kick the brake off and shove the cart, turning it into a rolling battering ram that will smash into anything in its path._
    
- **The Rubble:** The outer edges of the room are piled high with jagged rocks and discarded stone. _Mechanic: Counts as difficult terrain (costs double movement to walk through)._
    
- **The Alchemical Lanterns:** The three green lanterns provide dim light. _Mechanic: A player can shoot a lantern to plunge that section of the room into total darkness, or shatter it over a Husk to deal 1d4 acid/fire damage._
    

**Enemy Placement (The Iron Husks)**

- The **3 Iron Husks** are not clumped together; they are spaced out along the left and right walls, mindlessly swinging their heavy pickaxes into the quartz veins.
    
- **Their Behavior:** Because they are brain-dead cybernetic zombies, they have zero situational awareness. The deafening _CLANG_ of their own pickaxes makes them effectively deaf.
    
- **The Stealth Option:** As long as the players stay out of the direct green light of the lanterns and don't attack, they can literally sneak right down the center of the room, past the Husks, without triggering a fight!
    

**The Exits (Where do they go next?)**

- **To the Sides (Zone 3):** If the players inspect the left or right walls, they will notice thick, black chemical sludge slowly oozing out of a hairline fracture in the stone. Pushing on this section of the wall reveals the secret Dwarven supply closets (and the Black Oil Slimes).
    
- **To the Back (Zone 4):** At the far end of the 60-foot hall is a set of massive, heavy iron double doors. The Mandate has blown them half-open with explosives. Brilliant, prismatic light spills out from the gap, accompanied by the mechanical whining of the Cog-Mage's winch.
---

### **Zone 3: The Side Tunnels (The Toxic Runoff)**

"The air in this cramped, stone hallway is thick and burns the back of your throat. It smells of ozone, rot, and harsh chemicals. The Mandate’s drilling machines from the main hall have clearly ruptured something here; the stone floor is coated in a thick, bubbling layer of viscous black sludge that seems to almost reach out toward your boots as you step near it."

- **The Scene:** The floor is covered in a thick, bubbling black sludge that smells like sulfur and ozone.
    
- **Enemies:** **2 Black Oil Slimes** (Reskinned Ochre Jellies). They drop from the ceiling.
    
- **The Mechanic (Crucial):** If a slime takes **Slashing** or **Lightning** damage, it splits into two smaller, faster slimes!
    

**LOOT** - 100gp worth of jewels

---

### **Zone 4: The Core (The Boss & The Motherlode)**

> You push through the final set of heavy stone doors and are hit by a blinding, prismatic light. The entire back half of this cavern is a massive, hollow geode made of flawless, glowing quartz crystals. Suspended in the center of the room by a heavy mechanical harness is a Mandate [[Cog-mages|Officer]] in a scorched brass trench coat. She is operating a whining, gear-driven winch, violently trying to rip a crystal the size of a man straight out of the cavern wall. Around the room are some more undead digging away at ore veins."

- **The Scene:** A Mandate **Cog-Mage** (Imperial Engineer) is standing in the center of the room, using a heavy mechanical harness to try and pry a massive quartz crystal out of the wall.

### **The Interaction (How he reacts to them)**

**1. The Interruption** Unless the players immediately attack, let them fan out into the room. When they finally speak up or step into his peripheral vision, he doesn't jump in surprise. He simply pulls a lever to lock the winch in place. The deafening whining stops, leaving a sudden, ringing silence in the cavern. He slowly turns to look at them, adjusting the clicking, multi-layered lenses of his thermal goggles.

**2. His Demeanor (The Annoyed Scientist)** He looks them up and down—taking in their muddy Lord's Alliance armor, their weapons, and the fact that they clearly aren't Mandate soldiers. He sighs deeply, rubbing the bridge of his nose like a teacher dealing with unruly toddlers.

> **Cog-Mage:** _"Do you have any idea how incredibly delicate this chronal calibration is? The resonance frequency in this chamber is fluctuating by three percent just from you tracking mud across the threshold."_

**3. The Dialogue Tree (How he responds to the players)** Let your players talk. They might try to threaten him, bluff him, or demand answers. Here is how he counters:

- **If the players try to Bluff ("We're the new Vanguard squad / We were sent to help"):** He taps a gauge on his wrist. _"Incorrect. My chronal-ping shows the Vanguard squad outside flatlined four minutes ago. Which means you are either highly inefficient mercenaries, or leftover Alliance insurgents. Either way, you are trespassing on Imperial property."_
    
- **If the players demand to know what the Mandate wants with the quartz:** He actually smirks, showing a flash of ego. _"You look at this and see a shiny rock to sell in a tavern. We look at this and see the fuel for an era of absolute order. This single geode has enough chronal density to power a Dreadnought for a decade. Not that a barbarian with a rusty sword could comprehend the math."_
    
- **If the players threaten him ("Leave the dwarves' mine or die"):** He doesn't get angry. He just looks bored. _"The Eternal Mandate does not negotiate with unregistered combatants. You are an anomaly in the equation. And anomalies are to be erased."_
    

### **The Trigger (The Transition to Combat)**

The Cog-Mage will only talk for about a minute. He has a schedule to keep, and quartz quotas to meet.

When the players inevitably say something defiant, or when he simply decides he has wasted enough time on them, he turns his back to them and grabs the levers of his winch again.

> **Cog-Mage:** _(Without looking back at the party)_ _"Husks. The biologicals are contaminating the workspace. Vent the room."_

That is when the two Iron Husks drop their chains, draw their pickaxes, and turn their dead, glowing eyes toward the players. **"Roll for Initiative."**




- **The Loot (On the Worktable):** A velvet-lined Imperial Lockbox containing:
    
    - **150 gp worth of cut gems:** Two rubies cut into optical laser lenses, one sapphire carved into a frictionless gear.
        
    - **Tinker's Tools:** High-quality Mandate steel tools.
        
    - **Thermal Goggles:** (Reskinned _Goggles of Night_). Clicking, multi-layered glass lenses that grant 60 ft Darkvision.
        

---

### **Zone 5: The Exit (The Escape Sequence)**

The moment the players pry the giant quartz crystal free (or defeat the boss), the chronal energy holding the cavern together destabilizes. The room shakes violently, and the mine begins to collapse!

- **The Mechanic:** Run a quick **Skill Challenge** to escape the collapsing dungeon. Go around the table and ask each player how they navigate the crumbling tunnels.
    
    - _Examples:_ Athletics to hold up a falling beam, Acrobatics to dodge rocks, Survival to find the fastest path through the dust, or a spell like _Shatter_ to blast through a blocked exit.
        
- **The Consequence:** If a player fails their roll, they take **1d6 bludgeoning damage** from falling debris as they dive out of the canyon entrance.
    
- **The Resolution:** They burst out into the canyon just as the mine seals shut behind them in a plume of dust. Dazlyn and Norbus gladly pay the 100 gold bounty, thrilled that the Mandate is crushed—even if they have to dig the mine out all over again.
