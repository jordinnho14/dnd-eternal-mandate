### **The Event: The Blackout Cask (The Secret Cellar Feast)**

**The Hook:** A Mandate patrol just rolled through town and imposed a strict sundown curfew. But as the players are sitting in their dark, quiet rooms at the Inn, Toblen slips a note under their door: _"Cellar. 10 minutes. Don't let the armor clink."_

> **Read-Aloud:** *"You creep down the narrow wooden stairs into the root cellar of the Stonehill Inn. The heavy oak door shuts behind you, cutting off the howling wind outside.
> 
> Down here, it is a completely different world. Thick wool blankets are nailed over the tiny ground-level windows to block the light. The cellar is packed shoulder-to-shoulder with the people of Phandalin. Elmar Barthen is passing around carved wooden mugs; Qelline the halfling is carving a massive, illegally hunted roast boar; and Toblen is standing atop a barrel of smuggled Dwarven Stout, hammering the tap in with a mallet. For the first time since you arrived in this town, you hear people genuinely laughing."*

### **Three Mini-Games / Interactions (To let the players shine)**

When you run this, just let the players mingle. Throw these three specific moments at them to let them bond with the locals:

**1. The Miner's Challenge (For the Fighter/Barbarian)**

- A massive, soot-stained miner named Thistle walks up, slams a wooden mug on a barrel, and challenges your strongest player to an arm wrestle.
    
- **The Mechanic:** Best two-out-of-three contested **Strength (Athletics)** checks. If the player wins, the miners erupt in cheers, slap them on the back, and hand them a "trophy" (a beautifully carved, worthless piece of quartz they dug up).
    

**2. Carp's Secret Request (For the Caster/Bard)**

- Little Carp tugs on the robes of your magic-user and pulls them behind a stack of flour sacks. With him are three other wide-eyed town kids.
    
- **The Interaction:** Carp whispers, _"My dad said the Mandate took all the magic away. But... you guys are heroes. Can you do some? Just a little bit? We won't tell."_
    
- **The Payoff:** Let your player use a simple cantrip like _Prestidigitation_, _Minor Illusion_, or _Thaumaturgy_ to make a glowing spark, change a color, or make a tiny dragon illusion. The kids' minds will be absolutely blown, and they will treat that player like a literal god for the rest of the campaign.
    

**3. Halia's Good Stuff (For the Rogue/Charisma character)**

- Halia Thornton is sitting in the dark corner, not drinking the cheap ale. She waves your smoothest player over and pours them a shot of something amber from a crystal flask.
    
- **The Interaction:** _"Waterdeep whiskey. Smuggled it past a Mandate checkpoint in a cart full of manure. Drink up."_ It’s a quiet moment where she treats the player not as a pawn, but as an equal in the resistance.

### **The Emotional Anchor**

End the night with Toblen raising a mug. He doesn't give a grand, epic speech. He just looks at the players, looks at the townsfolk hiding in the dirt cellar, and says, _"To the Vanguard. And to staying alive one more week."_ By running this 20-minute roleplay event, you cement Phandalin as a warm, defiant family. When the Mandate eventually kicks that cellar door in during a later session, your players will unleash absolute hell to protect these people.
