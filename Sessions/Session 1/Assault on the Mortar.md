
### **1. The Scene & The Briefing**

Pip explains the situation. 
- Our job is to take out a mortar that is firing down at alliance troops in the valley
- asked his uncle for advice, suggested 3 possible courses of action
	- Sneak up a flooded drainage ditch to the mortar's blind spot - great if we get through unnoticed, but we will be knee deep in water if they spot us first
	- Shock and awe, just run straight at em - advantage on initiative
	- We could climb the slippery rocks to the right for a vantage point, and launch a ranged attack


- **Option A: The Mud Trench (Stealth):** Sneak up the flooded drainage ditch to the mortar's blind spot.
    - _Mechanic:_ Group Stealth Check (DC 12). Success = A full Surprise Round. Failure = Caught in difficult terrain when combat starts.
        
- **Option B: The Embankment (Shock & Awe):** Scream and charge straight up the exposed hill.
    - _Mechanic:_ Party gets Advantage on Initiative. If the first player kills an enemy in one hit, a free Intimidation check makes 1d4 conscripts instantly flee in terror.
        
- **Option C: The High Ground (Sniper):** Climb the slippery rocks to the right for a vantage point.
    
    - _Mechanic:_ Athletics check to climb. Ranged attackers get Half-Cover (+2 AC) and clear line of sight to the boss. Melee fighters must spend their first turn dashing down to the mortar.
        

### **2. The Scene

*"In the center of the plateau sits the Mandate mortar, and it is a monstrosity of brutal, ugly industry. It isn't a sleek cannon; it looks like a locomotive engine bolted upright onto a heavy iron turntable. The barrel is a massive, soot-stained iron tube pointing up at the sky, hooked into a network of violently hissing steam pipes and heavy brass pressure valves. At the base, a furnace door glows with a toxic, unnatural green heat.*"

Around it they see
- 4 goblins, frantically carrying glowing green shells
- 3 kobolds, 2 of them shoveling coal into the boiler, another bigger one whipping them when they slow down
- a hobgoblin operating the firing mechanism
- Another goblin, wearing an oversized officer cap, screaming orders at the group
	

### **3. The Ticking Clock (The Mortar)**

The players must break the machine before it wipes out their friends below.

- **Initiative Count 20 (Losing all ties):** If the Goblin Boss is alive/uninterrupted, he pulls the lever. _Describe a deafening THOOM, a blinding green flash, and thirty Alliance soldiers getting vaporized in the valley below._
    
- **How to Break It (3 Options):**
    
    1. **Brute Force:** Attack the boiler directly (AC 15, 40 HP).
        
    2. **Sabotage:** Use an Action to make a DC 13 Skill Check (e.g., _Sleight of Hand_ for valves, _Athletics_ for gears, _Arcana_ for the shells). **3 Successes break it.**
        
    3. **The Ammo Pile:** Shoot a fire spell or drop a torch onto the glowing green mortar shells stacked 10 feet away to trigger a chain reaction.
