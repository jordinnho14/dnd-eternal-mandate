# Stage 1: The Checkpoint (Social / Infiltration)

**Location:** The base of the winding mountain path leading up to the Shrine of Savras. **Objective:** Bypass the Mandate security perimeter without triggering a massive alarm.

#### Read-Aloud Description

> _"The narrow, muddy mountain road leading up into the foothills is completely gridlocked with idling merchant carts.  Ahead, blocking the only pass up the mountain, the Mandate has erected a temporary, militarized toll-gate. Thick iron barricades span the road. Two massive, smog-belching Tread-Crawlers are parked on either side, these big black constructions that look a bit like train engines, but mounted on treads, their heavy repeating-crossbow turrets swiveling lazily. A dozen Mandate Shock-Troopers are systematically ripping apart the civilian wagons, tossing cabbages and crates into the mud, searching for 'illegal technology.'"_
> *In the centre of the area where the shock troopers are searching, they've erected this like silvery metal pylon, with a pulsating glowing green orb at the top. Next to the pylon stands what looks like the leader of the operation, a larger man with thin glasses and a balding head, holding a clipboard which he keeps referring to.

*_As you observe the checkpoint from the back of the line, an elderly merchant in a battered wagon is waved forward by the commander. "Move it through," Kael barks._

_The old man snaps his reins, and his cart rolls between the two heavy Tread-Crawlers. Suddenly, the green Scrutiny Pylon in the center of the road shrieks—a piercing, mechanical whine. The glowing green crystal at its peak flashes violent red._

_Instantly, the heavy crossbow turrets on both Tread-Crawlers loudly click, swiveling to aim directly at the old man's chest. Two Shock-Troopers rip him down from his wagon, throwing him into the mud. One of them searches his pockets, pulling out a small, glowing blue potion of healing._ > _"Unregistered magical contraband," Kael sighs, stamping a piece of parchment. "Confiscate the wagon. Throw him in the holding pen."_

_As the crying old man is dragged away into the woods, the line of carts shuffles forward. There's a farmer near to you—a man sitting on a wagon full of turnips—watches this happen and begins to sweat profusely, visibly shaking._ *

#### The Moving Pieces (NPCs)

- **Lieutenant "Squeaky" Kael:** The officer in charge, stuffed into armor that is slightly too big for him. 
	- **Goal:** To finish his shift without any incidents so Prefect Rictus doesn't yell at him. 
	- **Flaw:** He is a cowardly bureaucrat. If threatened with overwhelming authority or a "Mandate Audit," he will panic and wave people through to avoid the headache.
    
- **12 Mandate Shock-Troopers:** The grunts shaking down the carts.
	- **Goal:** To bully civilians and steal shiny contraband. 
	- **Flaw:** They are bored and undisciplined. If something loud or weird happens nearby, they will abandon their posts to go look at it.
    
- **Barnaby Plumb (The Turnip Farmer):** A local farmer sweating profusely at the front of the line. 
	- **Goal:** To get through the gate without the Scrutiny Pylon detecting his his lullaby stone. Tap it twice -> glows with a dim light and plays a quiet lullaby. It was enchanted by his late wife, every night when the mandate rumble past their house, it scares his daughter, but the nightlight comforts her. But magic is fading -> he wants to take it to Shrine of Silvanus to ask for his blessing on the stone. -> if they help him he gives them a jug of turnip moonshine (basically a double strength potion of healing)
    
#### The "If/Then" Reactions (How the Sandbox Responds)

- **If the players use openly aggressive magic or weapons:**
	- Merchants panic and scatter.
	- The Mandate soldiers dive behind Tread-Crawlers for cover and man the crossbow turrets.
    
- **If the players try to sneak through the flanking woods:** 
	- Brush is  dry and crunchy. 
	- contested Stealth checks against the guards' Passive Perception.
	- Fail -> the guards don't shoot immediately -> send 2 guys to investigate
    
- **If the players create a massive distraction (explosions, stampeding horses):**
	- Kael loses control of the situation. 
	- The Scrutiny Pylon is temporarily ignored as the guards rush to fix situation
	- Small gap when unguarded
    
- **If the Scrutiny Pylon goes off:** 
	- The turrets automatically swivel and lock onto whoever triggered it.
	- Lieutenant Kael demands they step forward and submit to a full strip-search and confiscation.

## Stage 2: The Acoustic Minefield

* **MAKE SURE PLUM HAS LEFT THEM --- You** leave the checkpoint behind, and make your way further up the rocky mountain pass, which narrows into almost like a gorge that you're in the base of. The air is quiet up here, no sign of anyone living. There are Mandate posters on the walls of the canyon sometimes - an imposing likeness of Imperator Voss with his finger to his lips, and the line 'A well oiled machine does not squeak.' You come to a part of the valley where you can see ahead of you that nailed to the walls and loosely buried in the ground every 20 feet are these metallic disks, with faintly blinking red lights, and curled up dormant between them are these 4 massive wolf-like constructs, with no eyes on their heads, but they do have these huge like brass ear trumpets coming from the top of them.*

#### The Physical Board

- **The Terrain Hazard:** The ground is covered in loose scree (gravel) and dry autumn leaves. Moving at normal speed requires a **DC 13 Stealth check**. (Players can move at half-speed to gain Advantage on this check).
    
- **The Environmental Loophole:** There is a roaring waterfall on the far side of the canyon. The noise masks footsteps perfectly, but the rocks over there are slick with moss (requires Acrobatics to not slip and fall).
    
- **The Decoys:** There are wild canyon-goats grazing further up the ridge, and plenty of loose rocks the players could throw to create distant noises.
    

#### The Moving Pieces (The Hazards)

- **The Acoustic Mines:** These don't trigger on proximity; they trigger on decibels. A spell with a Verbal component, clanking heavy armor (rolling below a 13 on Stealth), or talking at normal volume will trigger a mine within 15 feet.
    
    - _The "Tick" Mechanic:_ They do not explode instantly. When triggered, the mine pops open, glows violently red, and emits a rising, mechanical whine. The players have exactly **one round** to react before it detonates (15-foot radius, 3d6 Thunder damage, and it wakes the Hounds).
        
- **3 Clockwork Hounds:** Blind, robotic tracking dogs. **Goal:** To sleep until a loud noise activates them, then hunt the source. **Flaw:** They literally cannot see. If the party stays perfectly still and silent, the hounds will walk right past them.
    

#### The "If/Then" Reactions (How the Sandbox Responds)

- **If a mine is triggered (The "Tick"):** Time slows down. Ask the player who triggered it: _"The mine at your feet opens and starts whining. You have three seconds. What do you do?"_
    
    - _If they throw a heavy blanket/bedroll over it:_ It muffles the blast. No damage, Hounds don't wake up.
        
    - _If they use Thieves' Tools:_ A quick DC 15 Sleight of Hand check snips the wire.
        
    - _If they cast Silence:_ The mine detonates, but the spell completely absorbs the sound and the damage.
        
    - _If they just run:_ They dive out of the radius, but the canyon echoes with a massive BOOM, and the Hounds immediately activate.
        
- **If the Paladin/Fighter in Heavy Armor keeps failing Stealth:** Let them realize they are a liability. A smart party might decide to put the Paladin inside a _Bag of Holding_ for 5 minutes, or cast _Pass Without Trace_, or physically carry them!
    
- **If the players throw a rock to trigger a mine on purpose:** The mine explodes, and all three Clockwork Hounds instantly sprint toward the explosion, leaving the path completely unguarded for the party to sneak by.
    
- **If a fight breaks out with the Hounds:** The Hounds will howl, triggering _more_ Acoustic Mines around them. The combat becomes a terrifying game of positioning, where players try to shove the Hounds into the blast radius of their own mines!

**ACOUSTIC MINE**
    15 foot radius sphere, dc 13 dex saving throw - fail take 3d6 thunder damage, save = half. Deafened for 1 minute.

### Stage 3: The DJ Arena (The Siege of the Shrine)

**Location:** The ruined courtyard of the Shrine of Savras. **Objective:** Break the anti-magic siege, defeat the Mandate Commander, and free Static.

#### Read-Aloud Description

> _"You crest the final ridge, and the silence of the canyon is violently replaced by the deafening sound of lute-punk music echoing from massive speakers. In the center of the ruined courtyard stands an ancient stone bell tower. The heavy iron doors are barricaded from the inside. At the top of the tower, leaning out the window with a microphone, is a Tiefling with copper wire wrapped around her horns, laughing and broadcasting live."_
> 
> _"Surrounding the tower are three massive, humming iron monoliths—Chronal-Jammers. They project a thick, glowing green laser between each other, forming a massive triangle around the tower. Outside the triangle, a Mandate Cog-Mage Commander furiously orders a squad of Shock-Troopers to batter down the door. Over the music, the Tiefling's voice booms across the courtyard: 'Looks like the Empire brought a battering ram! Too bad they didn't bring any rhythm!'"_

#### The Physical Board

- **The Anti-Magic Triangle:** The three Jammers form a glowing green perimeter. Any creature inside the triangle is in a "Null-Zone." Spells absolutely cannot be cast inside the triangle, active magical effects are suppressed, and magic weapons deal normal damage.
    
- **The Jammers:** Each Jammer is heavy iron (AC 15, 20 HP). They are mounted on rotating base plates. As an Action, a player can make a **DC 13 Athletics check** to physically shove a Jammer and rotate it 90 degrees, which completely changes the shape of the Anti-Magic Triangle on the battlefield!
    
- **The Tower Door:** The Mandate is currently hitting it with a heavy ram. It has about 3 rounds before it breaks open.
    
#### The Moving Pieces (NPCs)

- **Static (The Hype Woman):** Safe in the tower (for now). **Goal:** To humiliate the Mandate on live radio. **Mechanic:** At Initiative Count 20, she announces a "Bounty" (see below).
    
- **Cog-Mage Vex (The Boss):** A Mandate spellcaster hovering on a mechanical disk. **Goal:** To silence the broadcast at all costs. **Flaw:** He relies entirely on his tech-magic. He purposefully stays _outside_ the triangle. If the players rotate a Jammer so the Null-Zone covers him, his hover-disk immediately crashes to the ground and he cannot cast spells!
    
- **6 Shock-Troopers:** The grunts on the battering ram. **Goal:** Break the door. **Flaw:** They are currently inside the Null-Zone, meaning they can't be hit by the party's _Fireballs_, but they also can't receive magical healing or buffs.
    

#### Custom Mechanic: "Live Broadcast Bounties"

1. 'First person to drop a bucket-head using nothing but their fists or cold-hard steel is gonna end up as healthy as a horse' --  **first kill with melee attack gains 1d8 temp hp**
2. 'All this ground-pounding is putting my listeners to sleep - the next person to leap off somewhere high and hit a guy from above, you're getting a sonic speed-boost from me!' -- **attack an enemy jumping off high surface gets a free attack**
3. 'Those ugly jammers are really cramping my style, first person to blow one up and I'll chuck a sonic counter-shield to your posiition! They've got lightning flowing through them, maybe pump up the volume on that! - **Destroying a jammer or hitting it with lightning gives adv on next saving throw**
4. 'Hey don't just fight these fools, let's totally embarrass them! Shove a bloke in the dirt, and I'll hit anyone you want with my stage lights, full blast' -- **If they knock a soldier prone she will blind another one**

    

#### The "If/Then" Reactions (How the Sandbox Responds)

- **If a player rotates a Jammer:** The green laser sweeps across the battlefield. Any Mandate soldier caught in the new Null-Zone loses their glowing chronal-shields (lowering their AC by 2).
    
- **If a Jammer is destroyed (Drops to 0 HP):** It explodes in a 10-foot radius (2d6 Lightning damage). The triangle is broken, and the Anti-Magic field drops completely. The Cog-Mage panics and starts throwing his biggest spells.
    
- **If the players rotate all 3 Jammers to point inward at each other:** A catastrophic feedback loop occurs. All three Jammers detonate simultaneously in a massive, cinematic explosion that wipes out any remaining grunts in the center of the courtyard.
    
- **If the Tower Door breaks:** The grunts flood into the bottom floor. The players now have a ticking clock (about 3 rounds) to get in there before the grunts climb the stairs and reach Static!
    

### Stage 4: The Downhill Getaway (Skill Challenge)

**Location:** The winding, treacherous mountain road back to the valley floor. **Objective:** Survive the descent and outrun the Mandate reinforcements.

#### 1. The Setup (Read-Aloud)

> _"As the final Jammer explodes in a shower of green sparks, the valley below erupts with the sound of Mandate sirens. A massive Clockwork Gunship begins rising from the foothill checkpoint, its spotlight sweeping the mountain. Static kicks open the heavy wooden doors of the shrine's garage to reveal a stolen Mandate transport cart. She has crudely bolted two massive, glowing green chronal-thrusters to the back of it."_
> 
> _"Static tosses her microphone into the passenger seat, grabs the steering wheel, and screams over the sirens, 'Get in! We are punching a hole right through their airspace! Hold onto something!'"_

#### 2. The Meta-Drop (Explaining the Rules)

_Drop character and explain the mini-game to the table:_

> _"Alright everyone, we are entering a Skill Challenge. We are going to roll Initiative, but this is not a combat. You are rocketing down a mountain road at 80 miles an hour. As a team, you need to get **5 Successes before you hit 3 Failures** to escape."_ _"On your turn, I will throw an obstacle at you. You can use any skill on your character sheet, or cast a spell, to overcome it—but you have to justify how it helps, and you cannot use the same skill two rounds in a row. Let's roll Initiative!"_

#### 3. The Mechanics

- **The Target:** 5 Successes before 3 Failures.
    
- **Base DC:** 14 for all skill checks.
    
- **Magic Rule:** Cantrips require a spellcasting ability check (DC 14). Burning a Leveled Spell slot is an **automatic success**.
    
- **Failing Forward:** If a player fails a roll, the cart doesn't stop. They overcome the obstacle, but clumsily. **The entire party takes 1d6 bludgeoning damage** as the cart violently swerves, scrapes a wall, or takes fire. Mark down 1 Failure.
    

#### 4. The Hazards Roster

_(Go down the Initiative order and throw one of these at the active player. Ask: "What do you do?")_

- **The Gunship Spotlight:** The massive ship above locks its blinding spotlight onto the cart, winding up its crossbows. _(Solutions: Ranged attack to shoot the light, Fog Cloud, Deception to reflect light)._
    
- **The Hairpin Turn:** The cart is going 80 mph toward a sheer cliff drop-off. _(Solutions: Athletics to yank the wheel, Shape Water for an ice bank, Survival to lean into the turn)._
    
- **Boarding Hounds:** Two Scrutiny Hounds leap from the cliffs, landing on the roof of the cart. _(Solutions: Melee attack to knock them off, Acrobatics to kick them, Thunderwave)._
    
- **Engine Overload:** The crude chronal-thrusters overheat, threatening to explode. _(Solutions: Sleight of Hand/Tinker's Tools to hotwire the valve, Ray of Frost to cool the block)._
    
- **The Mandate Barricade:** Shock-Troopers at the bottom of a switchback push a heavy iron wagon across the road. _(Solutions: Shatter spell, Athletics to brace for impact, Minor Illusion to fake a swerve)._
    
- **The Innocent Bystander:** A terrified local goat-herder and his flock are frozen in the middle of the road. _(Solutions: Animal Handling to part the herd, Intimidation to scream at them, Acrobatics to drift)._
    
- **Ridge Snipers:** Mandate crossbowmen on the high ground rain bolts down on the open cart. _(Solutions: Perception to spot the glints and yell a warning, Strength to hold up a physical shield)._
    
- **The Tunnel Thread:** The road narrows into a tight rock tunnel that the cart will _barely_ fit through. _(Solutions: Survival to judge dimensions, Grease spell to lubricate the cart)._
    

#### 5. The Resolution

- **If they hit 3 Failures (The Crash):** The cart gets shot out from under them. They violently crash-land at the base of the mountain. They survive and escape into the woods, but everyone takes 3d6 bludgeoning damage and suffers 1 level of Exhaustion.
    
- **If they hit 5 Successes (Victory! Read-Aloud):**
    

> _"Static slams her foot on the chronal-thruster pedal. The cart goes airborne, launching directly over the heads of the Mandate blockade at the bottom of the hill. You smash down onto the dirt road of the valley floor, leaving a trail of green fire and confused Shock-Troopers in your wake. The Gunship above loses your signal in the treeline. Static lets out a massive whoop of victory, slapping the dashboard. 'Next stop, Phandalin! Drinks are on me!'"_
