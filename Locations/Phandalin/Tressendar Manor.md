### Description
*The muddy path winds up the eastern hill, away from the relative safety of town, until you stand before the looming silhouette of Tresendar Manor. Right now, it looks less like a grand estate and more like a decaying corpse.*

*The heavy stone foundations are still stubbornly intact, but the roof has entirely caved in on the west wing. The windows are nothing but jagged, empty holes that whistle like missing teeth as the freezing wind howls through them. Thick, thorny vines choke the stonework, and the front doors are completely gone, rotted away to splinters.*

*You step inside the main hall. It smells of wet ash, mildew, and generations of neglect. A family of fat raccoons scatters into the shadows as your boots crunch over broken floorboards. But as you look around... past the grime, past the puddles of rainwater pooling on the floor, you see the bones of something. It could be a warm, cozy home; an intimidating fortress; a hub of commerce and trade, or whatever you make of it. There is a massive stone hearth that could roar with fire, a sweeping staircase leading to a sturdy second floor, and heavy oak beams that have survived a century.*

*It is drafty, it is miserable, and it is freezing. But for the first time in two years... it's your*s.


### Layout
##### Ground Floor: The Bones of the Base

- **The Foyer & Main Hall:**
    - _The Vibe:_ Drafty and entirely exposed to the outside. The front doors are gone.
    - _Features:_ A grand, sweeping stone staircase dominates the room, leading up to the second-floor balcony. Underneath the stairs, hidden in the shadows, is **Halia’s reinforced iron door** with the brand-new lock.
    
- **The Great Room (East Wing):**
    - _The Vibe:_ Cold, damp, and cavernous. This is the heart of the manor.
    - _Features:_ A massive, floor-to-ceiling stone hearth sits on the far wall (currently clogged with debris). The floorboards here are mostly intact, though covered in raccoon tracks. _(This is where the 'Grand Hearth' upgrade will go!)_
    
- **The Kitchens (Back of the House):**
    - _The Vibe:_ Smells of old grease and mildew.
    - _Features:_ A rusted iron stove sits in the corner. The heavy wooden door to the walk-in pantry has been violently shredded from the inside by massive claws.

- **The Collapsed West Wing (The Courtyard):**
    - _The Vibe:_ Complete devastation.
    - _Features:_ The roof here completely caved in decades ago. It is essentially an open-air courtyard filled with shattered stone, thorny vines, and pooling rainwater. _(This is the perfect blank canvas for them to build the 'Armory' or Adabra's 'Apothecary Greenhouse' later!)_
        

---

### **Second Floor: The Living Quarters**

- **The Balcony Landing:** * _The Vibe:_ Precarious. The railing has rotted away, meaning one wrong step sends you falling 15 feet back down into the muddy Foyer.

- **The Master Suite (Above the Great Room):**
    - _The Vibe:_ Surprisingly intact, but completely barren.
    - _Features:_ A large, circular room with tall (broken) windows overlooking the town of Phandalin below. It has built-in, rotting wooden bookshelves lining the walls. _(Prime real estate for the 'Gnomish Library & Map Room' upgrade!)_
    
- **The Guest Quarters (The Party's Bedrooms):**
    - _The Vibe:_ Miserable and freezing.
    - _Features:_ Four smaller adjoining rooms. The beds are long gone, leaving only rusted iron frames and piles of wet, molding straw. The wind howls straight through the walls here. _(They will desperately want to buy the 'Featherbed' upgrade after sleeping here once)._
    
- **The West Wing Drop-Off:**
    - If they try to walk to the second floor of the West Wing, they can't. The hallway simply ends in a jagged drop-off where the floor collapsed into the rubble below.
        

---

### **The Basement: The Danger Zone**

- **The Entrance:** Down in the Foyer, a brand new, double locked iron door leads into the darkness
- **The Vibe:** If a player presses their ear to the iron door, they don't hear rats. They hear a faint, unnatural clicking sound, and they smell the sharp, metallic tang of raw, unstable magic wafting up through the floorboards.
