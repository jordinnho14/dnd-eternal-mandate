# Phandalin Shops & Inventories

## Adabra Gwyn's Apothecary Cart (The "Herbalist")
Because the Mandate executes magic users, Adabra cannot openly sell "Potions." She operates out of a wooden cart near the town square, loudly advertising her goods as "All-Natural Root Tinctures" and "Mineral Supplements." If a Mandate guard inspects her cart, it just looks like foul-smelling herbalism.

## Elmar Barthen's Provisions (The "General Store")
Barthen’s shop is the largest in town. The front of the store is perfectly legal, painfully boring, and heavily audited by the Mandate Scrutiny Eyes. He sells basic farming supplies, grain, and salted meats. However, Barthen is a secret Vanguard sympathizer. If the players prove they are rebels, he takes them into the back storeroom to show them the "good stuff."

# Location: The Dwarven Smithy (Dazlyn & Norbus)

Once the players clear the Dwarven Excavation, Dazlyn and Norbus move into the abandoned smithy in Phandalin. Because the Eternal Mandate strictly rations metal, the dwarves disguise their weapon-smithing as "industrial mining tool repair." If the players bring them scrap metal from destroyed Clockwork Hounds or Iron Husks, the dwarves will use it to forge heavy, anti-armor gear.

## Standard Stock (The "Mining Equipment")
These items are kept out in the open. If the Mandate inspects the shop, the dwarves claim these are just heavy tools for breaking rocks and clearing forests.

## Custom Forging & Upgrades (Vanguard Only)
The dwarves are master smiths. If the players leave a weapon or set of armor with them for **2 days** and pay the fee, Dazlyn and Norbus can modify their gear to help them fight the Mandate.

| Upgrade | Cost & Materials | Mechanics / Effect |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Armor-Cracking Weight** | 50 gp (Applies to Bludgeoning/Slashing weapons) | The dwarves add dense tungsten cores to the weapon. On a Critical Hit against a Construct or Armored enemy, you dent their plating, permanently reducing their AC by 1. |
| **Insulated Padding** | 100 gp (Applies to Medium/Heavy Armor) | The dwarves weave thick rubber scavenged from Mandate pipes into the armor's underlayer. The wearer gains Resistance to Lightning damage (perfect against Shock-Troopers). |
| **Iron Husk Scrap-Plate** | 750 gp + The chassis of a dead Iron Husk | The dwarves forge a custom suit of Half-Plate (AC 15 + Dex max 2) entirely out of scavenged Mandate armor. It looks terrifying and grants Advantage on Intimidation checks against Mandate Grunts. |

## Roleplay Notes
* **The Metal Snobs:** Dazlyn and Norbus absolutely despise the Mandate, but not for political reasons—they hate them because Mandate clockwork steel is "mass-produced, soulless, factory-stamped garbage." They constantly complain about the poor metallurgy of the enemies the players are fighting.
* **The Kobold Rivalry:** If you let the Kobolds squat in the Abandoned Smithy before the dwarves arrive, Dazlyn and Norbus are going to be furious when they move in. They will constantly complain to the players that they keep finding "lizard traps" in their coal bins and that their favorite hammers keep going missing.
